Keck-HISPEC

HISPEC (High-resolution Infrared Spectrograph for Exoplanet Characterization), slated for first light at W.M. Keck Observatory in 2026, is a new high-resolution (R~100,000) near-infrared (NIR) fiber-fed diffraction-limited spectrograph operating between 0.98-2.46 Î¼m (y, J, H, and K bands simultaneously). HISPEC led out of Caltech, the University of California (UC Los Angeles and UC San Diego) with participation from multiple institutions, including the Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL), the AstroBiology Center (Tokyo, Japan), the University of Montreal, the American Museum of Natural History (AMNH). For more information about HISPEC, please contact Prof. Dimitri Mawet (dmawet@astro.caltech.edu).
